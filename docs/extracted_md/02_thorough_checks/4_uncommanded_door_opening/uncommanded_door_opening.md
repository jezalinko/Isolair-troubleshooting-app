# 4. Uncommanded Door Opening

**System:** Fire Tank Hydraulic System  
**Fault Condition:** Doors open without command

## Possible Causes
- Hydraulic pressure bleed-off during high thermal load
- Faulty Jettison Solenoid coil
- Intermittent Cyclic Controller input
- Internal leakage or sticking Valve Cartridge (workshop only)

---

> **NOTE**
>
> **Preconditions / Assumptions**
> - Aircraft grounded
> - System accessible
> - Hydraulic Pump, Pressure Switch, and Relay checks completed
> - Fault occurs without a commanded jettison or door-open input

---

## 4.1 — Hydraulic Pressure Behaviour

**Purpose:**  
Determine whether door opening is caused by pressure decay rather than an electrical or mechanical fault.

### Observation
- Monitor hydraulic pressure during extended ground operation or high ambient temperature conditions.
- Observe whether pressure decays to the Pressure Switch cut-in point.

> **NOTE**
>
> During prolonged operation, hydraulic fluid temperature may rise, causing pressure to decay.
> Momentary Pressure Switch cut-in to restore pressure is **normal** and does **not** indicate a fault.

#### Observation Method
- System ON
- Doors closed
- Observe Pressure Gauge behaviour over time

- **Pressure decay followed by brief pump run, doors remain closed**
  - Normal operation
  - No fault found

- **Doors open without commanded input**
  - Proceed to **4.2 — Jettison Solenoid Check**

## 4.2 — Jettison Solenoid Check

**Purpose:**  
Verify that the Jettison Solenoid is not being unintentionally energised or mechanically held open.

---

### 4.2.1 — Solenoid Energisation

**Question:**  
Is the Jettison Solenoid coil energised when the doors open without command?

#### Test Method
- Monitor voltage at the Jettison Solenoid coil during the uncommanded door opening event.

Refer to **Pic xx**  
*(Image to be updated.)*

---

#### Result

- **YES — Coil energised**
  - Electrical command present
  - Proceed to **4.3 — Control Logic Verification**

- **NO — Coil not energised**
  - Proceed to **4.2.2 — Solenoid Coil Condition**

---

### 4.2.2 — Solenoid Coil Condition

**Question:**  
Is the Jettison Solenoid coil mechanically sticking or intermittently failing?

#### Test Method
- Inspect solenoid coil for:
  - Heat damage
  - Contamination
  - Intermittent operation
- Replace coil with known serviceable spare if in doubt.

Refer to **Pic xx**  
*(Image to be updated.)*

---

#### Result

- **Fault found**
  - Replace solenoid coil
  - Re-test system

- **No fault found**
  - Proceed to **4.3 — Control Logic Verification**

> **NOTE**
>
> Solenoid coils are **field-replaceable**.  
> Valve Cartridges are **not** field-replaceable.

---

## 4.3 — Control Logic Verification  
*(Cyclic Controller Inputs)*

**Purpose:**  
Verify that no unintended control signal is being generated by the Cyclic Controller.

### Checks
- Door Switch input
- Jettison Switch input
- Associated wiring continuity and insulation

#### Test Method
- Monitor Cyclic Controller outputs during uncommanded door opening.
- Verify no spurious ground or voltage is present on the jettison control line.

Refer to **Pic xx**  
*(Image to be updated.)*

---

### Result

- **Unintended command detected**
  - Replace Cyclic Controller with serviceable unit
  - Re-test system

- **No unintended command**
  - Proceed to **4.4 — Valve Cartridge Assessment**

---

## 4.4 — Valve Cartridge Assessment  
*(Workshop Only)*

**Purpose:**  
Determine whether internal hydraulic leakage or a sticking Valve Cartridge is allowing door movement.

> **CAUTION**
>
> Valve Cartridge replacement is **workshop only**.  
> Spare Valve Cartridges are **not** carried in field spares kits and must be issued from stores.

#### Assessment Method
- Remove and inspect Valve Cartridge for:
  - Internal leakage
  - Contamination
  - Mechanical sticking

- **Fault found**

  - Re-test full operation
