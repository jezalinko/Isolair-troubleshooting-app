4. UNCOMMANDED DOOR OPENING
Possible Causes:
• Overheating Jettison Valve
• Faulty Jettison Switch
• Faulty Doors Switch

4.1 - QUICK CHECK
4.1.1 – An uncommanded door opening can be caused by a number of issues, all which have
their own fault characteristics. All descriptions below are explained as it would occur IF the
tank were carrying a load of water.
1. All doors relax very slowly causing the water to flow out at a very slow rate. Doors
may not even seem to open.
This is normally caused by the Jettison Valve Solenoid overheating, causing it to slowly relax
and allowing the hydraulic pressure to gradually drop. You may find that the Amber LED on
the Fire Tank Control Box will be flashing or there will be excess current draw on the
ammeter, due to the Hydraulic Pump running as the pressure drops. Even though the
Hydraulic Pump will run, the pressure may not build in the system. This does not mean that
the solenoid is damaged or faulty!
The Jettison Valve Solenoid can over heat due to the constant power at the Solenoid Coil,
which is provided to energise the Solenoid Valve allowing it to hold the pressure in the
system.  The solenoid coil may require airflow to keep it cool enough to operate correctly.
This is most likely to occur whilst hot re-fuelling on a hot day with the system left on.
If this does occur try turning the system off while hot re-fuelling, or if it occurs during water
bombing operations, try turning the system off (either via the Jettison Switch or the main
power on the Isolair Display Box) whenever flying WITHOUT a load of water to allow the air
to cool the solenoid.
If this doesn’t help the problem, the Jettison Solenoid may not be getting a sufficient ground
or power. Continue with ‘Jettison Solenoid Check’. (para. 4.2.1)

2. All doors open as per a Jettison Release – open suddenly, but doors slowly retract
as the tank empties.
The Jettison Switch is a double pole, double throw switch. Meaning it has two sets of
contacts, both operated by a single switch. It is possible that as a Jettison Switch begins to
fail, it could intermittently lose one of either connections or both connections inside the
switch, preventing power from getting to - the Jettison Valve Solenoid, the Hydraulic Pump
Relay, or both.
A loss of connection/power to the Jettison Valve Solenoid ONLY, will cause the Jettison
Valve to relax whilst there is no connection/power (this will cause hydraulic pressure to
drop, therefor making the Hydraulic Pump to run and causing the Amber Light on the Fire
Tank Control Box to continue flashing) If this occurs with a load of water on-board, the
doors will open under the weight of the water. As the tank empties, the doors will slowly
retract due to the Door Return Springs. – THIS DOES NOT MEAN THAT THE DOORS HAVE
CLOSED AND THE TANK IS WORKING NORMALLY.
If the tank is empty when the fault occurs, then it may not be easily noticeable to the pilot,
except for the ammeter which will indicate to the pilot the there is excessive current draw,
and the Amber Light on the Fire Tank Control Box will continue to flash as the Hydraulic
Pump continues to run.
A total Jettison Switch failure (both contacts) will cause the hydraulic pressure to drop, but
the Hydraulic Pump will NOT run, as there is also no connection to the Hydraulic Pump
relay.
If this fault occurs during flight, toggle the Jettison Switch a few times to see if the problem
stops as it may just be intermittent. If so, ensure that the Cyclic Controller is changed ASAP –
during next fuel cycle and replace faulty Jettison Switch in the original Cyclic Controller as
soon as possible.
If the problem persists during water bombing operations, the pilot should land as soon as
possible and investigate further.
When doing further testing for the problem on the ground, in all cases as above, continue
with ‘Jettison Switch Check’ (para. 4.3.1)
* A loss of connection in the Jettison Switch contact to the Hydraulic Pump relay should not
cause the doors to relax. As the system will initially not turn on if the Hydraulic Pump does
not run, and this problem will cause other issues instead. If this occurs, change the Cyclic
Controller, and repair the Jettison Switch on the original Cyclic Controller as soon as possible.

3. Only the selected doors will open as per a normal water drop, but DO NOT retract
once emptied.
The Door Switch is a double pole, double throw switch. Meaning it has two sets of contacts
both operated by a single switch. It is possible that as a Door Switch begins to fail, it could
short out intermittently therefore making a faulty contact to either one of, or both
connections inside the switch. One contact in the switch goes to the Cross-over Valve
Solenoid, the other goes to the Hydraulic Pump relay.
If there is a faulty connection made ONLY between the contact that go to the Cross-over
Solenoid, then there will be power to the Cross-over Solenoid, causing the Cross-over Valve
to energise. This will not cause any immediate problems. But, if the hydraulic pressure drops
below 850psi (as it may possibly bleed off), the Pressure Switch will ‘cut in’, causing the
Hydraulic Pump to run. As the Cross-over will be energized, this will force the flow of
hydraulic fluid to reverse, causing the Doors to open. As the hydraulic pressure will not
increase and climb into the normal operating range, the pump will continue to run, and the
doors will remain open. There will be no control over the doors.
A total Door Switch short (both contacts) will simulate a door drop, (Cross-over Valve and
Hydraulic Pump relay energised) but as the there is no control over the Doors, the doors will
remain open.
If this fault occurs during flight, toggle the Doors Switch to see if the problem stops as it may
be intermittent. If so, ensure that the Cyclic Controller is changed ASAP – during next fuel
cycle and replace faulty Doors Switch in the original Cyclic controller as soon as possible.
If toggling the Doors Switch does not immediately prevent the problem, use the Jettison
Switch to cut power to the system and the doors will retract via the Door Return Springs.


If the doors remain open in any situation, you must use EXTREME CAUTION when required
to land, as severe damage may be caused to the tank due to the doors contacting the
ground. If using the Jettison does not allow the doors to retract, use the Power ON/OFF
switch on the side of the Isolair Display box to cut power to the system WITH THE
JETTISON STILL IN THE EMERGECNY POSITION (DOWN)
If the problem persists, the pilot should land as soon as possible and investigate further.
When doing further testing for the problem on the ground, in all cases as above, continue
with ‘Doors Switch Check’ (para. 4.4.1)
*If there is a faulty connection made ONLY between the contacts that go to the hydraulic
pump relay, then the doors will not open. Although, it will cause the Hydraulic Pump to
continue running. If this occurs, change the Cyclic Controller, and repair the Doors Switch on
the original Cyclic Controller as soon as possible.
CAUTION!!!

4.2 - JETTISON SOLENOID CHECK
4.2.1 - Q - Is there a ground and power (24v-28v) at the relevant wires of the Jettison
Solenoid Coil when the system is turned on?
Remove either the cap from the back of the Jettison Solenoid coil, or the two wires that
connect directly to the Solenoid Coil terminals, and check for both a ground and power at
each respective wire or terminal inside the cap. (see pic 41)
Yes- Wiring is fine. Replace the cap or wires, and check again. If pump continues to run and
the hydraulic pressure does not build, it is likely that the Jettison Solenoid or Jettison Valve
is faulty. Continue to check Jettison Solenoid/Valve. (para. 4.2.2)
No- No Power - Continue with ‘Jettison Switch Check’. (para. 4.3.1)
       No ground – Check ground wiring to Jettison Solenoid Coil.
Pic 41





4.2.2 - Q – Does the Jettison Valve work when another Solenoid Coil is used?
Swap the Solenoid Coil with one of the Door Actuator Solenoid Valve Coils (or a spare in the
spares kit) and retry the system. (see pic 42)
Yes – Faulty coil. Replace coil with a new/spare coil. If there is no new/spare coil, then use
the Door Actuator Solenoid Valve Coil from the door that is most likely to remain OPEN for
each drop.
No – Most likely a faulty Jettison Valve. Change the Jettison Valve with a new valve. DO NOT
interchange the shaft section of the valve with a Door Actuator Valve shaft, as they are
slightly different and it will not operate properly. (see pic 43)

4.3 - JETTISON SWITCH CHECK
If the Hydraulic Pump continues to run but the pressure does not build in the system
(indicated by the Pressure Gauge), then it’s likely that the Jettison Switch is faulty.
4.3.1 – Q – Is there continuity between pins 9 and 5 on the Cyclic Controller plug (small DB9
connector) when the Jettison Switch is in the normal position? (see pic 44)
Power is supplied to the Jettison switch from pin 9, then out to the Jettison Solenoid via pin
5. There must be continuity between these pins when the Jettison Switch is in the normal
position (up). (see Diagram 6.1).
Yes – If the connection is broken when the Jettison Switch is pulled down into the
Emergency position (off) then the Jettison Switch is fine. Continue with question below
(para. 4.3.2)
No – Jettison Switch is faulty. Replace Cyclic Controller and re-check system. Replace faulty
switch as soon as possible.
Pic 44





4.3.2 – Q – Is there power (24v-28v) at pin ‘G’ on the Milspec 24 pin Data Plug RECEPTACLE
with the system turned on?
Power to the Jettison Valve Solenoid is supplied via pin ‘G’ on the Milspec 24 pin Data Plug
RECEPTACLE located under the pilot door. (see pic 23)
Yes – Power supply for the Jettison Valve up to pin ‘G’ is fine. Continue below. (para 4.3.3)
No – Either a faulty Isolair Display Box or a faulty wire from pin ‘19’ in the large DB25
connector (connected to Isolair Display Box) to pin ‘G’ in the Milspec 24 pin data plug
RECEPTACLE. Change the Display Box and re-try system. If this does not work, check for
continuity between pin ’19’ of the large DB25 connector to slot ‘G’ of the Milspec 24 pin
Data PLUG. (see Diagram 6.2 - Isolair Display Box)

4.3.3 - Q - Is there continuity between pin ‘G’ on the 24 pin Milspec data PLUG and the
power supply terminal of the Jettison Valve Solenoid?
Check continuity from pin ‘G’ on the Milspec 24 pin Data PLUG to the power terminal on the
Jettison Valve Solenoid by removing BOTH wires off the Jettison Solenoid Coil.
 Yes – Power supply to the Jettison should be fine, but if there is still no power present at
the Jettison Solenoid, there is a likely problem with the data plug and receptacle connection
- check this connection. Also ensure that the Jettison Valve Solenoid has a good ground. If all
wiring checks out fine and the problem still exists, investigate Jettison Solenoid and Jettison
Valve further, continue with ‘Jettison Solenoid Check’ (para. 4.2.2)
No – There is a problem with the wiring from pin ‘G’ on the 24 pin Milspec plug, and the
Jettison Valve Solenoid. Check this wire/connection. (see Diagrams 6.3, 6.4 or 6.5)

4.4 – DOOR SWITCH CHECK
4.4.1 - Q- Is there continuity between pins 9 and 2 on the Cyclic Controller plug (small DB9
connector) when the Doors Switch is relaxed (not triggered)? (see pic 44)
Yes- The Door Switch in the Cyclic Controller is faulty. Replace Cyclic Controller, and replace
faulty switch as soon as possible.
No- Door Switch is fine. Continue with ‘Hydraulic Cross-over’ Check. (para 4.5.1)

4.5 – HYDRAULIC CROSS-OVER CHECK
4.5.1 – Q- Is there power (24v-28v) to the Hydraulic Cross-over when the system is on?
(ensure that the Doors Switch is NOT triggered)
There should not be power to the Hydraulic Cross-over at any time unless the Doors Switch
is triggered.
Yes – If the Doors Switch was found to be serviceable, then power must be getting to the
Hydraulic Cross-over via a short from another source. Use the Wiring Diagrams to
investigate where the power is shorting.
