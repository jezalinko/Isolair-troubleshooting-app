## Pressure Switch Check

**System:** Hydraulic Pump Continues To Run  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 44–46)

---

## Purpose
Determine whether the Pressure Switch is incorrectly set or faulty and causing continuous Hydraulic Pump operation.

---

## Applicability / Configuration

> **NOTE**
>
> All current tank installations use **Stauff Pressure Switches only**.  
> Earlier Isolair-manufactured pressure switches are no longer used or supported.
>
> Current Stauff Pressure Switches do **not** allow individual control wires to be removed for isolation testing.  
> Troubleshooting must therefore be carried out using **hydraulic pressure readings**, not wire-removal tests.

---

## Step 1 — Hydraulic Pressure Check

**Question:**  
What is the hydraulic pressure when the Hydraulic Pump is running continuously?

### Test Method
- Ensure the system is ON.
- Observe the **Hydraulic Pressure Gauge** while the Hydraulic Pump is running.
- Record the steady-state pressure reading.

Refer to **Pic 24**.

---

### Result

- **HIGH — Pressure above 1200 psi**
  - The system is over-pressurised.
  - Likely causes:
    - Pressure Switch incorrectly set or faulty
    - Hydraulic Pump Power Relay fault
    - Doors Switch fault
  - Proceed to **Step 2 — Pressure Switch Evaluation**.

- **MEDIUM — Pressure between 850–1200 psi**
  - The Hydraulic Pump output pressure may be incorrectly set.
  - Adjust the Hydraulic Pump output pressure in accordance with  
    **Hydraulic Pump Output Pressure Adjustment (para. 3.8.1)**.
  - After adjustment:
    - Normal operating pressure should stabilise at **850–1150 psi**.
    - The Hydraulic Pump should stop running.
  - If the pump continues to run, proceed to **Step 2 — Pressure Switch Evaluation**.

- **LOW — Little or no hydraulic pressure**
  - The Pressure Switch is unlikely to be the cause.
  - Likely causes:
    - Jettison Solenoid / Valve fault
    - Jettison Switch fault
  - Proceed to **Jettison Solenoid Check (para. 3.5.1)**.

---

## Step 2 — Pressure Switch Evaluation

> **NOTE**
>
> Pressure Switches are frequently misdiagnosed as faulty when the actual issue is incorrect pressure setting.

- Do **not** discard removed Pressure Switches without inspection.
- If pressure readings indicate incorrect switching behaviour:
  - Check Pressure Switch cut-in and cut-out settings.
  - Re-adjust the Pressure Switch in accordance with  
    **Pressure Switch Adjustment (para. 3.9)**.
- If correct adjustment cannot be achieved:
  - Replace the Pressure Switch with the approved **Stauff replacement unit**.
  - Seal all terminals and caps against moisture ingress using **DC4 silicone grease**.
  - Re-test system operation.

---

## Next Action

- If the Pressure Switch is confirmed serviceable and correctly set →  
  Proceed to **Hydraulic Pump Power Relay Check**.
- If the Pressure Switch was adjusted or replaced →  
  Re-test the system before continuing troubleshooting.
