## Pressure Switch Adjustment

**System:** Hydraulic Pump Continues To Run  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 54–58)

---

## Purpose
Adjust the Pressure Switch to ensure the hydraulic system maintains the correct normal operating pressure and prevents continuous Hydraulic Pump operation.

---

## Description

The Pressure Switch controls the **normal operating pressure** of the hydraulic system.

- **Normal operating range:** 850–1150 psi  
- **Typical steady pressure:** ~1000–1100 psi  
- **Required cut-in pressure:** 850 psi  

If hydraulic pressure drops below the cut-in setting, the Pressure Switch will momentarily run the Hydraulic Pump to restore system pressure.

> **NOTE**
>
> This pressure correction occurs very briefly and may not be noticeable unless the Pressure Gauge is observed during ground testing.

---

## When Adjustment Is Required

- Hydraulic pressure stabilises **below 850 psi**
- Hydraulic Pump continues to run
- Pressure behaviour indicates incorrect Pressure Switch setting
- Troubleshooting indicates Pressure Switch is not operating at the correct cut-in point

---

## Step 1 — Determining Pressure Switch Cut-In Setting

**Question:**  
At what pressure does the Hydraulic Pump momentarily run to restore system pressure?

### Test Method
- Place a **clean container** beneath a hydraulic hose connection near the Pressure Switch.
- With the system **ON**, slowly loosen (“crack”) the hydraulic fitting to allow hydraulic fluid to flow slowly into the container.
- Observe the Pressure Gauge as pressure bleeds off.

Refer to **Pic 34**.

---

### Observation
- As pressure decreases, the Hydraulic Pump will run briefly in **short bursts** to restore pressure.
- Adjust the fitting as required so the pump runs approximately **every 3–4 seconds**.
- This bleed rate allows an accurate cut-in pressure reading.

---

### Result

- The pressure at which the pump momentarily runs is the **Pressure Switch cut-in setting**.
- Acceptable cut-in pressure:
  - **No less than 850 psi**
  - **No greater than approximately 900 psi**

Refer to **Pic 35**.

---

### Decision

- **Cut-in within limits →**
  - Pressure Switch setting is acceptable.
  - No adjustment required.

- **Cut-in outside limits →**
  - Pressure Switch must be re-set.
  - Proceed to **Step 2 — Setting the Pressure Switch**.

---

## Step 2 — Setting the Pressure Switch

> **CAUTION**
>
> Pressure Switches must remain installed on the hydraulic system during adjustment.  
> Images showing removed switches are for instructional purposes only.

### Adjustment Preparation
- Ensure the system is **OFF**.
- Remove the **locking screw** from the end of the Pressure Switch using a **2.5 mm Allen key**.

Refer to **Pic 37**.

---

### Adjustment Method
- Position a **clean container** beneath a hydraulic fitting near the Pressure Switch.
- With the system **ON**, slowly crack the hydraulic fitting to allow controlled pressure bleed-off.

Refer to **Pic 38**.

- Observe the Pressure Gauge as pressure decreases.
- Allow the Hydraulic Pump to run in short bursts (every 3–4 seconds).
- Identify the current cut-in pressure.

Refer to **Pic 39**.

---

### Pressure Adjustment
- Insert a **large flat-blade screwdriver** or **Dzus fastener screwdriver (preferred)** into the Pressure Adjusting Screw.
- Adjust while pressure is bleeding off:
  - **Clockwise (IN):** Increases cut-in pressure
  - **Anti-clockwise (OUT):** Decreases cut-in pressure
- Slowly adjust until the Hydraulic Pump consistently cuts in at **850 psi**.

Refer to **Pic 40**.

---

## Completion
- Turn the system **OFF**.
- Refit the **locking screw** securely.
- Tighten the hydraulic fitting used to bleed pressure.
- Remove the container and clean any residual fluid.
- Turn the system **ON** and confirm:
  - Hydraulic pressure stabilises within **850–1150 psi**
  - Hydraulic Pump does **not** continue to run

---

## Next Action

- If correct pressure behaviour is achieved →  
  Return the system to service.
- If correct cut-in pressure cannot be achieved →  
  Replace the Pressure Switch and re-test system operation.
