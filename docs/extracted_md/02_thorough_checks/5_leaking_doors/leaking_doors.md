## 5.0 Purpose
Identify the cause of leaking tank doors and determine whether corrective action is required.

---

## 5.1 Applicability

> **NOTE**
>
> This procedure is a **workshop-level inspection**.
> Door adjustment must only be carried out by personnel trained in tank door rigging.

---

## 5.2 Description

Tank doors may leak due to:
- Seal wear after extended use
- Contamination from poor water quality
- Incorrect door cable tension

---

## 5.3 Preparation

> **CAUTION**
>
> Ensure **aircraft power is OFF** before accessing tank doors.
> Tank doors are fitted with return springs and will resist opening.

- Position the aircraft **on wheels** to allow the tank to tilt back for easier access.
- Carefully pry the doors open, maintaining control against spring force.

Refer to **Pic 46**.

---

## 5.4 Door Seal Inspection

**Question:**  
Are the door seals damaged or missing?

### Inspection Criteria
- Minor surface tearing along the seal path can be normal.
- Seals are considered serviceable if:
  - No section is missing
  - No foreign material is embedded in the seal
- Seals are unserviceable if:
  - Sections are torn away
  - Material is missing
  - Seal is displaced or hardened

Refer to **Pic xx**  
*(Image to be updated.)*

---

### Result

- **Seals unserviceable**
  - Replace the affected seal section using items from the **Fire Tank Spares Kit**,  
    or replace the entire seal if required.
  - **Tank removal from the aircraft is required** for seal replacement.
  - Re-test door sealing after replacement.

- **Seals serviceable**
  - Proceed to **5.5 — Door Cable Assessment**.

---

## 5.5 Door Cable Assessment

**Question:**  
Is incorrect cable tension contributing to door leakage?

- Uneven leakage may indicate insufficient cable tension on one section of the door.

> **CAUTION**
>
> Do **not** attempt to adjust door cables without proper training.
> Incorrect adjustment may worsen door sealing or cause additional faults.

Refer to **Pic xx**  
*(Image to be updated.)*

---

### Action

- If adjustment is required:
  - Ensure it is carried out by **trained personnel**, or
  - Have qualified personnel provide guidance before adjustment.

---

## 5.6 Next Action

- If leakage is resolved →  
  Return the system to service.
- If leakage persists →  
  Re-evaluate door seals, cable rigging, and door alignment.
