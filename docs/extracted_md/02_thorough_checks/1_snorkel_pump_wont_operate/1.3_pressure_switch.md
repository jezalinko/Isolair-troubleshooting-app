# 1.3 — Pressure Switch Check
**System:** Snorkel Pump Won’t Operate  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 19–20)

---

## Purpose
Verify that the pressure switch is supplying power to the snorkel pump control circuit when hydraulic pressure is within the normal operating range.

---

## Preconditions / Notes
- System ON  
- Hydraulic pressure within normal operating range (850–1150 psi)  
- **DOOR CLOSE** LED illuminated  

---

## Step 1 — Pressure Switch Output
**Question:** Is there 24–28 VDC at the **High Pressure (N/O)** terminal of the pressure switch when:
- The system is ON, and  
- The fault is present?

### Test Method
- With the system powered and the fault present:
  - Measure voltage at the **High Pressure (N/O)** terminal on the pressure switch.
  - On Stauff pressure switches, this is **Terminal 4**.
- Refer to **Pic 4**, **Pic 5**, and **Diagram 6.9** for terminal identification.

> When hydraulic pressure is in the normal operating range, the pressure switch closes the **High Pressure (N/O)** contacts, supplying power to the Snorkel Pump Power Relay.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - The pressure switch is functioning correctly.
  - Proceed to **1.4 — Snorkel Pump Power Relay Check**.

- **NO →**
  - The pressure switch is faulty or not closing correctly.
  - Replace the pressure switch.
  - Re-test the system.
  - When installing a replacement, ensure it is sealed against water ingress using **DC4 silicone grease**.

---

## Connector & Terminal Reference

- **Stauff Pressure Switch**
  - Terminal 4 — High Pressure (N/O)  
  - Terminal 1 — Common  
  - Terminal 2 — Low Pressure  
  - Ground — Ground terminal
