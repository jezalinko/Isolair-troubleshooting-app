1- SNORKEL PUMP WON’T OPERATE

Possible Causes:
• Faulty Pump Motor

• Faulty Pressure Switch

• Faulty Snorkel slave relay
• Faulty Snorkel power relay
• Faulty Cyclic Controller Pump switch
• Faulty Pump wiring

1.4.4 - Q - Is there power (24v-28v) to the respective small control terminal on the Snorkel
Pump Power Relay when the system is turned ON? (see pic 7)
There will be power provided to one of the control terminals on the Snorkel Pump Power
Relay from the Pressure Switch when ever the system is ON and the pressure is in the
normal operating range. Remove one of the control wires from the relay to ensure it is
tested correctly - as a false reading can be given through the relay coil if both control wires
are connected.
Yes- The Pressure Switch, and the connection from the Pressure Switch to the Snorkel Pump
Power Relay control terminal is fine. Continue below. (para. 1.4.5)
No- If the Pressure Switch was determined to be serviceable, then the connection from the
Pressure Switch to the Snorkel Pump Power Relay control terminal is faulty. Check the wire
between these two points (see Diagrams 6.4 or 6.5).
If the Pressure Switch has not been checked yet, check the serviceability of the Pressure
Switch. (para 1.3.1)

1.4.5 - Q - Is there a ground at the respective small control terminal of the Snorkel Pump
Power Relay only when the Pump Switch is triggered?
Once it’s determined which control terminal is supplied with power (as above - para. 1.4.4),
it must be determined if there is a ground present at the other control terminal when the
Pump Switch is triggered. (ensure one wire is still disconnected as per question above)
Yes- Pump Switch and connecting control wire are fine.
If it is determined that there is both power at one control terminal, and a ground at the
other control terminal when the Pump Switch is triggered, but there is still no power at the
large output terminal, then it is likely that the Snorkel Pump Power Relay is faulty. Change
Power relay and try again. (ensure to use a 200amp relay P/N - 24214)
No- There is either a problem with the Pump Switch or the wiring to the ground control
terminal on the Snorkel Pump Power Relay. Continue with ‘Cyclic Controller Pump Switch
Check’ (para. 1.6.1)

1.4.6 -

The following questions only apply to tanks that use Slave Relays to control the Snorkel
Pump Power Relay. (see pic 6 and Diagram 6.3)
Q - Is there a constant ground at the respective control terminal of the Snorkel Pump Power
Relay?
Check that there is a constant ground at one of the small control terminals. To correctly
identify which wire supplies the ground, either one of the control wires MUST be removed
before testing (this will ensure that a false reading is not given via the relay coil), or check
that there is approx. 0.5 ohm (to a suitable ground) at one terminal, and approx. 70 ohm (to
a suitable ground) at the other terminal. The terminal with approx. 0.5 ohm is the terminal
supplied with a constant ground. (see pics 7 and 8)
Yes- Ground control wire to Power Relay is fine. Continue below. (para. 1.4.7)
No- Problem with the ground wire to the Power Relay control terminal. Check for a problem
in the connection. The ground control wire should be connected to a common ground inside
the control box.

1.4.7 - Q - Is there power (24v-28v) at the other control terminal of the Snorkel Pump Power
relay when the Pump Switch is triggered?
Once it’s determined which control terminal is supplied with the constant ground, it must
be determined if the other terminal is supplied with power only when the system is ON and
the Pump Switch is triggered. (see pic 7 and 8). (Ensure one of the control wires from the
relay is still removed to ensure it is tested correctly - as a false reading can be given through
the relay coil.)
Yes- The control circuit that supplies power to the Snorkel Pump Power Relay control
terminal is fine. If it was determined that each respective control terminal has a ground and
power, and the Snorkel Pump Power Relay has no output power, it’s likely that the Power
Relay has failed. Replace the Power Relay and try again.
No- Problem with the control power circuit. Continue below with ‘Snorkel Pump Slave
Relay Check’. (para 1.5)

1.5 - SNORKEL PUMP SLAVE RELAY CHECK

All tanks SHOULD have the Slave Relays wired identically - as per the wiring diagrams.
Though, it is possible that terminals 85 and 86 are wired in reverse, or terminals 30 and 87
are wired in reverse. This will NOT affect the operation of the relay, but may hinder
troubleshooting. It is recommended that the correct wiring of the Slave Relay is
determined before carrying out each check. (see pic 6 and Diagram 6.3 -Original 214 Tank)

1.5.1 - Q – Is there power (24v-28v) at the Snorkel Pump Slave Relay control terminal when
the system is turned on?
If the Pressure Switch is serviceable (indicated by the green Doors Close LED illuminated on
Isolair Display Box), power is supplied to the Snorkel Pump Slave Relay at terminal 85 (86 if
wired in reverse) from the Pressure Switch (via the Cyclic Controller) when the system is on,
and the pressure is in the normal operating range. (see pics 1 and 6)
Yes- Continue to check Snorkel Pump Slave Relay. (Para. 1.5.3)
No- There may be a problem with the wiring from the Pressure Switch to the Snorkel Slave
Relay. Continue below. (para. 1.5.2)

1.5.2 – Q- Is there continuity between the high pressure contact on the Pressure Switch, and
the relevant terminal on the Snorkel Pump relay?
Check for continuity between either terminal 4 (Stauff Pressure Switch), or the red wire
(Isolair Pressure Switch), to terminal 85 (86 if wired in reverse) on the Snorkel Pump Slave
Relay (see pics 4 and 6)
Yes- Assuming the Pressure Switch is serviceable (indicated by the green Doors Close LED
illuminated on the Isolair Display Box), and there is continuity between the two points as
just determined, then there should be power at terminal 85 (86) when the system is turned
on and the hydraulic pressure is within the normal operating range. Re-check for power at
terminal 85 (86 if wired in reverse). If there is power present, continue below. (para 1.5.3) If
power is still not present, change the Pressure Switch and try again.
No- There is a fault in the wire that connects the high pressure terminal on the Pressure
Switch and the Snorkel Pump Slave Relay. Check this wire/connection. (see Diagram 6.3)

1.5.3 - Q - Is there a ground at the Snorkel Pump Slave Relay when the Pump Switch is

The Snorkel Pump Slave Relay should have a ground at terminal 86 (85 if in reverse) when
the Pump Switch is triggered. (see pic 6) To ensure you have the correct terminal, remove
the Slave Relay from the relay base for testing to ensure a false reading is not given through
the relay coil. Check for a ground at the wire/terminal in the relay base.
Yes- If it was determined that there is both power via the Pressure Switch, and a ground at
the correct terminals of the Snorkel Pump Slave Relay (85 and 86) when the system is on
and the Pump Switch is triggered, continue below. (Para. 1.5.4)
No- Problem with the Pump Switch or ground wiring on Pump Switch circuit. Continue with
‘Cyclic Controller Pump Switch Check’ (Para. 1.6)

1.5.4 - Q - Is there constant power (24v-28v) at terminal 30 of Snorkel Pump Slave Relay
when the system is turned ON?
Constant power should be supplied to terminal 30 (87 if in reverse) of the Snorkel Pump
Slave Relay when the system is turned on. (see pic 6) (see Diagram 6.3)
Yes- Continue below. (para 1.5.5)
No- Problem with the constant power supply to the Slave Relay terminal. (30 or 87)

1.5.5 - Q - Is the Slave Relay operating when the Pump Switch is triggered?

(30 or 87), and check that the other pin only has power when the Pump Switch is triggered.
(see pic 6) (see Diagram 6.3)
Yes- Snorkel Pump Slave Relay is fine. Continue below. (para 1.5.6)
No- Snorkel Pump Slave Relay is faulty. Replace Slave Relay and re-try.

1.6 - CYCLIC CONTROLLER PUMP SWITCH CHECK

The Pump Switch in the Cyclic Controller is a momentary switch that can be triggered either
a forward or backward direction to operate the Snorkel Pump. Both directions are on their
own separate circuit. It is possible that if the Pump Switch is faulty, one direction may work
while that other does not. Try to operate the Snorkel Pump by triggering the Pump Switch
both directions. (see pic 9) (Diagram 6.1 - Cyclic Controller)
If this does not work, replace the Cyclic Controller with the spare in the Fire Tank Spares Kit
and try again. If the Snorkel Pump now works, it is likely that the Pump Switch was faulty.
Temporarily setup the spare Cyclic Controller so the aircraft can continue to operate, and
repair the faulty Cyclic Controller Pump Switch as soon as possible to ensure that you
always have a spare serviceable Cyclic Controller.
If this does NOT fix the problem, continue below. (para 1.6.1)
