# 1.4 — Snorkel Pump Power Relay Check
**System:** Snorkel Pump Won’t Operate  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 20–22)

---

## Purpose
Verify that the Snorkel Pump Power Relay is receiving correct control signals and supplying power to the snorkel pump.

---

## Step 1 — Relay Output
**Question:** Is there 24–28 VDC at the **large output terminal** of the Snorkel Pump Power Relay **only when the Pump Switch is triggered**?

> When the Pump Switch is triggered, the relay should click inside the tank control box and supply power to the snorkel pump via the large output terminal.

Refer to **Pic 7**.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - The relay is switching correctly.
  - Check for power at the **Snorkel Pump Anderson Plug (214)**.
  - Ensure both Anderson plug contacts are fully locked in the housing (see **Pic 3**).
  - If no power is present at the Anderson plug, proceed to **Step 2 — Power Wire Continuity (1.4.2)**.

- **NO →**
  - The relay is not switching.
  - Proceed to **Step 3 — Relay Control Circuit (1.4.3)**.

---

## Step 2 — Power Wire Continuity (1.4.2)
**Question:** Is there continuity between the **large output terminal** on the relay and the **power pin** on the Anderson plug?

Refer to **Pic 2** and **Pic 7**.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - The power wire is serviceable.
  - Ensure the other Anderson plug terminal has a good ground.

- **NO →**
  - The power wire is faulty.
  - Inspect and repair wiring between the relay and the Anderson plug.

---

## Step 3 — Relay Type & Terminals (1.4.3)

**Relay type:** Britax / Bosch 24 V, 20 A (Snorkel Pump Power Relay)

| Terminal | Typical Function |
|--------|-----------------|
| 30 | Constant power input |
| 87 | Power output to snorkel pump |
| 85 | Control (power or ground) |
| 86 | Control (ground or power) |

> **NOTE:**  
> Terminals 30 and 87, and terminals 85 and 86, may be wired in reverse without affecting relay operation.  
> Always trace the **large gauge wires** to confirm which relay feeds the snorkel pump.

Refer to **Pic 7** and **Pic 8**.

---

## Step 4 — Control Power (1.4.4)
**Question:** Is there 24–28 VDC at one of the **small control terminals** when the system is ON and hydraulic pressure is in the normal range?

> This voltage is supplied from the **Pressure Switch**.  
> Remove one control wire when testing to avoid false readings through the relay coil.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - Pressure Switch and control feed to the relay are correct.
  - Proceed to **Step 5 — Control Ground (1.4.5)**.

- **NO →**
  - If the Pressure Switch has already been confirmed serviceable → check wiring between the Pressure Switch and the relay control terminal (see Diagrams 6.4 or 6.5).
  - If not yet checked → return to **1.3 — Pressure Switch Check**.

---

## Step 5 — Control Ground (1.4.5)
**Question:** Is there a ground at the other **small control terminal** when the Pump Switch is triggered?

(Leave one control wire disconnected while testing.)

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - Pump Switch and control wiring are functioning.
  - If there is power on one control terminal, ground on the other, but **no output on the large terminal**, the relay is faulty.
  - Replace the Snorkel Pump Power Relay with **P/N 24214 (200 A)** and re-test.

- **NO →**
  - Faulty Pump Switch or wiring between the Cyclic Controller and the relay control ground.
  - Proceed to **1.6 — Cyclic Controller Pump Switch Check**.

---

## Relay Identification

- **New-style 214 tank Snorkel Pump Power Relay**
- **Original-style 214 tank Snorkel Pump Power Relay**

> Always trace the **large gauge wires** to either the Hydraulic Pump or Snorkel Pump to confirm the correct relay before testing.
