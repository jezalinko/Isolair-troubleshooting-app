# 1.5 — Cyclic Controller Pump Switch Check
**System:** Snorkel Pump Won’t Operate  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 21–22)

---

## Purpose
Determine whether the Pump Switch within the Cyclic Controller is preventing the snorkel pump from operating.

> **Operational Policy**  
> In the field, the Cyclic Controller must be replaced as a complete unit.  
> Individual switch replacement is to be carried out **only by tank technicians in the workshop**.

---

## Step 1 — Pump Switch Function (Quick Check)
**Question:** Does the snorkel pump operate when the Pump Switch is triggered in **either direction**?

> The Pump Switch is a momentary switch with **two independent circuits** (forward and backward).  
> One direction may work even if the other is faulty.

Refer to **Pic 9** and **Diagram 6.1**.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - The Pump Switch is partially functional.
  - Continue to operate if required.
  - Replace the Cyclic Controller or repair the Pump Switch at the next maintenance opportunity.

- **NO →**
  - Replace the Cyclic Controller with the spare unit from the Fire Tank Spares Kit.
  - Re-test the system.

  - If the snorkel pump now operates:
    - The original Cyclic Controller is faulty.
    - Keep the spare installed so the aircraft remains serviceable.
    - Repair the faulty Pump Switch in the workshop as soon as possible to restore a spare controller.

  - If the snorkel pump still does not operate:
    - Proceed to **Step 2 — Cyclic Controller Output (1.5.1)**.

---

## Step 2 — Cyclic Controller Output (1.5.1)
**Question:** Is there continuity between **pins 6 and 8** on the small **DB9 connector** of the Cyclic Controller **only when the Pump Switch is triggered**?

### Test Method
- Disconnect the **DB9 connector** from the rear of the Isolair Display Box  
  (located behind the large DB25 connector).
- Trigger the Pump Switch and check continuity between **pins 6 and 8**.

Refer to **Pic 9** and **Diagram 6.1**.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - The Pump Switch and Cyclic Controller output are correct.
  - Proceed to **Step 3 — Data Plug Ground (1.5.2)**.

- **NO →**
  - Faulty Pump Switch or internal wiring within the Cyclic Controller.
  - Repair or replace the Pump Switch in the workshop to restore controller serviceability.

---

## Step 3 — Data Plug Ground (1.5.2)
**Question:** Is there a ground present at **slot ‘M’** on the **24-pin data plug receptacle** when the Pump Switch is triggered?

> The Isolair system must be powered ON.  
> The main power plug must remain connected.

### Test Method
- Disconnect the **24-pin data plug** from the aircraft.
- With the system ON, check for ground at **slot M** on the **receptacle** while triggering the Pump Switch.

Refer to **Pic 10** and **Diagram 6.1**.

---

### Result

- **NO →**
  - Fault with the Isolair Display Box or wiring to the data plug.
  - Check wiring diagrams or replace the Isolair Display Box (one spare at each base).

- **YES →**
  - Isolair Display Box and aircraft wiring are correct.
  - Proceed to **Step 4 — Tank Wiring (1.5.3)**.

---

## Step 4 — Tank Wiring (1.5.3)
**Question:** Is there continuity between **pin ‘M’** on the 24-pin data plug and the **ground control terminal** of the **Snorkel Pump Power Relay**?

> Slave relays are no longer fitted to McDermott Isolair tanks.  
> All testing must be done directly to the **Snorkel Pump Power Relay**.

Refer to **Pic 7**, **Pic 8**, and **Pic 11**.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - Tank wiring is correct.
  - Re-check Snorkel Pump Power Relay operation (Section 1.4).

- **NO →**
  - Faulty tank wiring or damaged pin in the 24-pin data plug.
  - Inspect wiring and connectors and repair as required (Diagrams 6.3, 6.4, 6.5).

---

## Connector Reference

- **Cyclic Controller**
  - Small **DB9** connector  
  - Large **DB25** connector

- **Tank Data Plug**
  - **24-pin Milspec receptacle**  
  - Pin **M** — Pump Switch ground return
