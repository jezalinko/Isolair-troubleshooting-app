# 1.3 — Pressure Switch Check
**System:** Snorkel Pump Won’t Operate  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 19–20)

---

## Purpose
Verify that the pressure switch is supplying power to the snorkel pump control circuit when hydraulic pressure is in the normal operating range.

---

## Step 1 — Pressure Switch Output
**Question:** Is there 24–28 VDC at the **High Pressure (N/O)** terminal of the pressure switch when:
- The system is ON, and  
- The fault is present?

> When the system is ON and hydraulic pressure is in the normal operating range, the pressure switch supplies power to the Snorkel Pump Power Relay.  
> Continuity should exist across the **High Pressure (N/O)** contacts, placing voltage on **Terminal 4** of the Stauff pressure switch.

Refer to **Pic 4**, **Pic 5**, and **Diagram 6.9** for terminal identification.

---

### Result

- **YES →**
  - The pressure switch is serviceable.
  - Proceed to **1.4 — Snorkel Pump Power Relay Check**.

- **NO →**
  - The pressure switch is likely faulty.
  - Replace the pressure switch.
  - Re-test the system.
  - When installing the replacement switch, ensure it is sealed against water ingress using **DC4 silicone grease**.

---

## Connector & Terminal Reference

- **Stauff Pressure Switch terminals**
  - Terminal 4 — High Pressure (N/O)
  - Terminal 1 — Common
  - Terminal 2 — Low Pressure
  - Ground — Ground terminal
