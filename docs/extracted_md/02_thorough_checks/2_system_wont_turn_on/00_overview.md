2. SYSTEM WON’T TURN ON (HYDRAULIC PUMP WON’T RUN)

Possible Causes:
• Circuit Breakers
• Faulty Cyclic Controller - Jettison Switch
  - Doors Switch
• Faulty Pressure Switch
• Faulty Hydraulic Pump Relays
• Faulty wiring
2.1 - QUICK CHECK
2.1.1 - Ensure that the ON/OFF switch on the Isolair Display Box is ON, and the two Circuit
Breakers on the side of the Isolair Display Box are IN (see pic 12). Next, ensure that the
Jettison Switch on the Cyclic Controller is in the normal operating position - UP (see pic 13).
(If the Isolair dual controller is installed, check the Jettison Switch on the dual controller as
well).
Once these checks are completed, check if there are any LED’s illuminated on the Isolair
Display Box (see pic 12). If there are LED’s illuminated (at a minimum, the FSC and Pump
Temp LED’s will illuminate) then there IS power to the system, but it is likely there is a
problem with the tank. Continue with ‘Cyclic Controller Check’ (para 2.3.1)
If no LED’s are illuminated, then there is NO power getting to the system. Continue with
‘Circuit
Breaker
Checks’
(para
2.2.1)



For the 214, if you are trying to operate the tank WITHOUT the engine running (from
ground power), ensure the ‘NON ESSENTIAL BUS’ is in the ‘Manual’ position (see pic 14).

2.3 - CYCLIC CONTROLLER CHECK
2.3.1 - Q - Does the system turn on when a spare Cyclic Controller is installed?
Install the spare Cyclic Controller and re-try the system - ensure that the Jettison Switch is in
the normal operating position - UP.
Yes – Faulty Cyclic Controller. It is possible that either the Jettison Switch or the Doors
Switch was faulty. Determine which switch was faulty and replace ASAP (see Diagram 6.1 -
Cyclic Controller to determine which switch is faulty). (Spare switches are found in the Fire
Tank Spares Kit)
No - Cyclic Controller most likely serviceable. Continue with ‘Tank Power Supply Check’
(para. 2.4.1)

2.4 - TANK POWER SUPPLY CHECK
2.4.1 - Q – Is there power (24v-28v) at the large power input terminal of the Hydraulic Pump
Power Relay when the system is turned on? (see pic’s 7 and 18)
Ensure you have the correct large terminal - trace it back to the Power Interconnect plug.
Yes – There is power to the tank, continue with ‘Pressure Switch Check’. (para. 2.5.1)

2.5 - PRESSURE SWITCH CHECK
2.5.1 - Q – Is there power (24v-28v) at the ‘common’ terminal of the Pressure Switch when
the system is turned on?
Determine what type of Pressure Switch is on the tank - Stauff or Isolair. (see pic 22) Ensure
that either the relevant wire remains on the terminal when checking, or if the wire is
removed for checking, check the tank wire that supplies power to the common terminal.
Stauff Pressure Switch - Common is terminal 1. (see pic 22 and Diagram 6.9)
Isolair Pressure Switch - Common is the black wire. (see pic 22 and Diagram 6.9)
Yes- Power supply OK, continue to check Pressure Switch below. (para. 2.5.2)
No- Problem is in the Isolair System or wiring. Continue by checking wiring. (para. 2.5.3)

2.5.2 - Q - Is there continuity between the ‘Normally Closed’ contacts (‘Common’ and the
‘Low Pressure’ terminals) in the in Pressure Switch?
When the system is turned OFF remove the *black* plug from the Stauff pressure switch, there should
be continuity between;
Stauff Pressure Switch- Terminals 1 and 2. (see pic 22 and Diagram 6.9)        or

Yes- If there is power at the ‘Common’ terminal, and there is continuity between the
‘Normally Closed’ contacts, then there is a problem either in the wiring from the Pressure
Switch to the Hydraulic Pump Power Relay, the Jettison Switch
or Door Switch, or the wiring between these points. First, CONFIRM that the Cyclic
Controller is serviceable (even if the Cyclic Controller has been checked as per 2.3.1) by
continuing with ‘Jettison Switch and Door Switch Check’. (para. 2.6.1)
No- Faulty Pressure Switch - replace. Ensure that the new Pressure Switch is well sealed to
prevent any water ingress. Use DC4 silicone grease.

2.5.3 - Q - Is there continuity between pin 25 on the large DB25 connector (bottom of the
Isolair Display Box) and the Pressure Switch common terminal? (see pic’s 9, 22 and 23)
Pin 25 from the Isolair display box provides power to the Pressure Switch. Ensure that the
24 pin data plug is connected to the aircraft and check for continuity between these two
points.
Stauff Pressure Switch - Common is terminal 1. (see pic 22 and Diagram 6.9) 
Yes- Wiring is fine. It is likely that there is an issue with the Isolair Display Box or Main Power
Relay assembly inside the aircraft. Change display box and try again. If this still does not
work, check the Main Power Relay Assembly- Diagram 6.6 - Isolair Display Box (Isolair
Drawing) or 6.10 - Main Power Relay Assembly.
No- There is a problem between these two points. Use wiring diagram to determine where
the problem is. (Ensure the aircraft/tank interconnect plugs are connected) (see Diagrams -
6.2, 6.3, 6.4 or 6.5)

2.6 - JETTISON SWITCH AND DOOR SWITCH CHECK
2.6.1 - Q - Is there continuity between pins 3 and 4 on the small DB9 connector (underneath
the Isolair display box) when Jettison Switch is in the ‘normal’ position (up)?
Control power to the Hydraulic Pump Relay is provided from the Pressure Switch, via the
Jettison and Door Switch’s on the Cyclic Controller, and through pins 3 and 4 on the small
DB9 connector. If both switches are fine, then there will be continuity between these two
pins. (see pic 9) (see Diagrams 6.1, 6.2, 6.3, 6.4, and 6.5)
Yes- Both switches are fine. Continue to check relays. See below (para. 2.6.2)
No- Faulty switch - Replace the Cyclic Controller to remain serviceable, then determine
which switch is faulty and replace it as soon as possible. (see Diagram 6.1 - Cyclic Controller
to determine which switch is faulty). (Spare switches are found in the Fire Tank Spares Kit)

2.6.2
> **NOTE**
>
> Earlier tank installations incorporated a Slave Relay to control the Hydraulic Pump Power Relay. This configuration is no longer in use.
>
> All current tank installations operate without Slave Relays, and Slave Relays have been removed from service across the fleet. References to Slave Relay identification, inspection, or troubleshooting in legacy documentation are therefore not applicable.

> **CAUTION**
>
> Do **not** attempt to identify, test, or reinstall Slave Relays on current tank installations. Such actions may result in incorrect troubleshooting, unnecessary component replacement, or configuration errors.

> **ACTION**
>
> Proceed directly with Hydraulic Pump Power Relay checks in accordance with **paragraph 2.7.1**.

2.7 - HYDRAULIC PUMP POWER RELAY CHECK
2.7.1 - Q – Is there power (24v-28v) at one of the control terminal wires on the Hydraulic
Pump Power Relay when the system is ON?
There should be power to one of the small control terminal of the Hydraulic Pump Power
Relay when the system is on (see pic 7). Remove one of the control wires from the power
relay to ensure it is tested correctly - as a false reading can be given through the relay coil if
both wires are connected.
Yes - Power control circuit to Hydraulic Pump Power Relay is fine. Continue below. (para
2.7.2)
No – If it was determined that there was power to the Pressure Switch ‘common terminal’,
and the Pressure Switch was serviceable, PLUS the Cyclic Controller was determined to be
serviceable, then there is a fault with one of the wiring connections between the Pressure
Switch ‘low pressure’ terminal, and the Hydraulic Pump Power Relay. Continue with Wiring
Continuity Checks below. (para. 2.8.5) (use Diagrams 6.1, 6.2, 6.4 or 6.5 to assist with
continuity checks).

2.7.2 - Q – Is there a ground at the other control terminal wire of the Hydraulic Pump Power
Relay?
There should be a constant ground at the other small control terminal on the Hydraulic
Pump Power Relay. (see pic’s 7 and 20) Ensure one control wire remains off the power relay
when testing for a ground.
Yes –– If there is both a ground at one control terminal, and power at the other control
terminal of the Hydraulic Pump Power Relay, and the Hydraulic Pump will still not run,
continue below. (para. 2.7.3)
No – Problem with control ground wire to the relay. Check for fault in the ground
wire/connection.

2.7.3 - Q - Is there power (24v-28v) on both large terminals of the Hydraulic Pump Power
Relay when the system is turned on?
If there is both a ground, and power to the control terminals of the Hydraulic Pump Power
Relay, and there is power at large input terminal, there should also be power at the large
output terminal if the relay is serviceable. (see pic 7) There should also be a noticeable
‘clicking’ noise coming from the Relay as it energises when the system is turned on.
Yes – The Hydraulic Pump Power Relay is fine, continue below. (para. 2.7.4)
No - The Hydraulic Pump Power Relay is faulty – replace and re-try. (ensure to use a 200amp
relay P/N - 24214)

2.7.4 - Q – Is there power (24v-28v) and ground at the respective terminals on the Hydraulic
Pump motor?
If the Hydraulic Pump Power Relay is working as normal, then there should be power
through to the Hydraulic Pump Motor. There should also be a constant ground to the other
terminal. (see pic 21)
Yes – If there is both a ground and power at the Hydraulic Motor but it is not running, then
the motor has failed. It will need to be either pulled apart for further investigation, or the
entire motor removed and replaced.
No - No power (24v-28v) – Check wiring from Hydraulic Pump Power Relay to Hydraulic
Pump.
        No Ground – Check ground wire from pump back to Tank Control Box, then through to
original source of the ground in the aircraft.
