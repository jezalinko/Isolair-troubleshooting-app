# First Check — HYDRAULIC PUMP CONTINUES TO RUN

**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf  
**Document:** McDav-FFTTG  
**Revision:** 03  
**Pages:** 15

---

## Symptom
- Hydraulic Pump continues to run

---

## Preconditions / Notes
- The most common problem is the Pressure Switch failing.

> **IMPORTANT:** Do not throw Pressure Switches away — they may only need re-adjusting.

---

## Decision Steps

### Step 1 — Hydraulic Pressure Check
**Question:** What is the Hydraulic Pressure? (Check Pressure Gauge)

Select the observed pressure range:

- **High (>1150 psi)** → Proceed to Step 2A  
- **Medium (850–1150 psi)** → Proceed to Step 2B  
- **Low (<850 psi, most likely ~50 psi)** → Proceed to Step 2C  

---

### Step 2A — High Pressure Path (>1150 psi)
**Action:** Change Pressure Switch  

**Question:** Does the Hydraulic Pump now operate properly?

- **YES →**
  - Pressure Switch was set too low  
  - DO NOT discard; re-set pressure  
  - See **Part 3.9 — Pressure Switch Adjustment**

- **NO →**
  - Re-check Hydraulic Pressure on the gauge  

  - **If pressure is below 1300 psi →**
    - Re-set Hydraulic Pump output pressure  
    - See **Part 3.8 — Hydraulic Pump Output Pressure Adjustment**

  - **If pressure is above 1300 psi →**
    - Possible faulty Hydraulic Pump relay  
    - Continue with **Para. 3.3.1 — Pressure Switch Check**

---

### Step 2B — Medium Pressure Path (850–1150 psi)
**Action:** Adjust Hydraulic Pump output pressure  
(See **Part 3.8 — Hydraulic Pump Output Pressure Adjustment**)

**Question:** Does the Hydraulic Pump now operate properly?

- **YES →**
  - Pressure was set too low  

- **NO →**
  - Proceed to **Step 2A — Pressure Switch Check**

---

### Step 2C — Low Pressure Path (<850 psi)
**Action:** Change Cyclic Controller  

**Question:** Does the Hydraulic Pump now operate properly?

- **YES →**
  - Most likely a faulty Jettison Switch  
  - Replace Cyclic Controller  

- **NO →**
  - Proceed to Step 3  

---

### Step 3 — Jettison Solenoid Electrical Check
**Question:** With system power ON, is there power and ground at the Jettison Solenoid terminals?  
*(At least one wire must be removed when checking)*

- **YES →**
  - Possibly a faulty Jettison Solenoid valve or coil  
  - Continue with **Para. 4.2.2 — Jettison Solenoid Check** (from Uncommanded Door Opening)

- **NO →**
  - Wiring fault suspected  
  - Continue with **Para. 3.5.1 — Jettison Solenoid Check**

---

## Outcomes & Diagnoses
- **Pressure Switch set incorrectly** — Re-adjust per **Para. 3.9**  
- **Faulty Pressure Switch** — Replace if adjustment fails  
- **Faulty Hydraulic Pump Relay** — Replace relay  
- **Faulty Jettison Switch** — Replace Cyclic Controller  
- **Faulty Jettison Solenoid** — Perform **Para. 4.2.2**  
- **Wiring fault** — Perform **Para. 3.5.1**
