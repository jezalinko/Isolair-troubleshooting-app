# First Check — UNCOMMANDED DOOR OPENING

**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf  
**Document:** McDav-FFTTG  
**Revision:** 03  
**Pages:** 16

---

## Symptom
- Firefighting tank doors open without command, or remain open

---

## Preconditions / Notes
> **CAUTION:** If the doors remain open in any situation, use extreme caution when landing to avoid damage to the doors or tank body.

---

## Decision Steps

### Step 1 — Door Position Check
**Question:** When the problem occurred, did the doors remain up in the closed position after the tank was emptied?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 2  
- **NO →** Proceed to Step 3  

---

### Step 2 — Door Opening Speed Check
**Question:**  
Did the doors open quickly like a normal Jettison release (high flow), or slowly like a severe door leak (low flow)?

- **High Flow (Jettison-like release) →** Proceed to Step 2B  
- **Low Flow (slow leak) →** Proceed to Step 2A  

---

### Step 2A — Jettison Solenoid Overheating
**Action:** Jettison Solenoid Check  

**Question:**  
If the doors opened slowly and water took much longer than normal to release, the Jettison Solenoid may have been overheating, causing the Jettison Valve to relax and hydraulic pressure to drop.  
Ensure the system is turned off during refuelling and between loads to allow the solenoid to cool.  
Does the system now operate correctly?

- **YES →**
  - Overheating Jettison Solenoid  

- **NO →**
  - Begin a more thorough check at **Para. 4.2.1 — Jettison Solenoid Check**

---

### Step 2B — Jettison Switch Path
**Action:** Possible faulty Jettison Switch  
Replace Cyclic Controller and re-test.

**Question:** Does the system now operate properly?

- **YES →**
  - Faulty Cyclic Controller (Jettison Switch)  

- **NO →**
  - Begin a more thorough check at **Para. 4.3.1 — Jettison Switch Check**

---

### Step 3 — Hydraulic Pump Running Check
**Question:** Does the Hydraulic Pump continue running and the door(s) remain open when the system is turned on?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 3A  

- **NO →**
  - If the door(s) remain open when the system is OFF, there is an internal door fault  
  - Must be investigated further  

---

### Step 3A — Door Switch Path
**Action:** Possible faulty Door Switch  
Replace Cyclic Controller and re-test.

**Question:** Does the system now operate properly?

- **YES →**
  - Faulty Cyclic Controller (Door Switch)  

- **NO →**
  - Cross-over valve possibly faulty  
  - Continue with **Para. 4.5.1 — Hydraulic Cross-over Check**

---

## Outcomes & Diagnoses
- **Faulty Door or Jettison Switch** — Replace Cyclic Controller  
- **Overheating or Faulty Jettison Solenoid** — Perform Para. 4.2.1  
- **Faulty Cross-over Valve** — Perform Para. 4.5.1  
- **Internal Door Fault** — Mechanical inspection required
