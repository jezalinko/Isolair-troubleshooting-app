# 1.1 — Quick Check
**System:** Snorkel Pump Won’t Operate  
**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf (pp. 17)

---

## Purpose
Verify that hydraulic pressure and door status are correct before testing components.

---

## Step 1 — Hydraulic Pressure
**Question:** Is the hydraulic pressure within the normal operating range  
(850–1150 psi on the Pressure Gauge)?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 2  
- **NO →** Proceed to Step 3  

---

## Step 2 — Door Status
**Question:** Is the green **DOOR CLOSE** LED illuminated on the Isolair Display Box?

> The DOOR CLOSE LED illuminates when hydraulic pressure is in the normal range and the doors are fully closed.  
> The snorkel pump will not operate unless the doors are closed.

- **YES →** Proceed to **1.2 — Snorkel Pump Motor Check**  
- **NO →** Proceed to **1.3 — Pressure Switch Check**

---

## Step 3 — Pressure Not in Range
If hydraulic pressure is not within 850–1150 psi:

→ Proceed to **1.3 — Pressure Switch Check**

---

## Visual References
- Pressure Gauge — Normal range 850–1150 psi
- Isolair Display Box — Green DOOR CLOSE LED
