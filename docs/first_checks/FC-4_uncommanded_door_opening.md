# First Check — UNCOMMANDED DOOR OPENING

**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf  
**Document:** McDav-FFTTG  
**Revision:** 03  
**Pages:** 16  

---

## Symptom
- Firefighting tank doors open without command, or remain open  

---

## Preconditions / Notes
> **CAUTION**  
> If the doors remain open in any situation, use extreme caution when landing to avoid damage to the doors or tank body.

---

## Decision Steps

### Step 1 — Door Position Check
**Question:**  
When the problem occurred, did the doors remain up in the closed position after the tank was emptied?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 2  
- **NO →** Proceed to Step 3  

---

### Step 2 — Door Opening Speed Check
**Question:**  
Did the doors open quickly like a normal Jettison release (high flow),  
or slowly like a severe door leak (low flow)?

- **High Flow (Jettison-like release) →** Proceed to Step 2B  
- **Low Flow (slow leak) →** Proceed to Step 2A  

---

### Step 2A — Jettison Solenoid Overheating Path
**Action:**  
Allow Jettison Solenoid to cool.

**Question:**  
If the doors opened slowly and water took much longer than normal to release, the Jettison Solenoid may have been overheating, causing the valve to relax and hydraulic pressure to drop.  
Ensure the system is turned off during refuelling and between loads.  
Does the system now operate correctly?

- **YES →**
  - Overheating Jettison Solenoid identified  

- **NO →**
  - Begin detailed troubleshooting at **Jettison Solenoid Check**

---

### Step 2B — Jettison Switch Path
**Action:**  
Replace Cyclic Controller and re-test.

**Question:**  
Does the system now operate correctly?

- **YES →**
  - Faulty Cyclic Controller (Jettison Switch)  

- **NO →**
  - Begin detailed troubleshooting at **Jettison Switch Check**

---

### Step 3 — Hydraulic Pump Running Check
**Question:**  
Does the Hydraulic Pump continue running and the door(s) remain open when the system is turned on?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 3A  

- **NO →**
  - If the door(s) remain open when the system is OFF, an internal door fault is likely  
  - Mechanical investigation required  

---

### Step 3A — Door Switch Path
**Action:**  
Replace Cyclic Controller and re-test.

**Question:**  
Does the system now operate correctly?

- **YES →**
  - Faulty Cyclic Controller (Door Switch)  

- **NO →**
  - Cross-over valve possibly faulty  
  - Begin detailed troubleshooting at **Hydraulic Cross-over Check**

---

## Outcomes & Diagnoses
- **Faulty Door Switch or Jettison Switch** — Replace Cyclic Controller  
- **Overheating or faulty Jettison Solenoid** — Perform Jettison Solenoid Check  
- **Faulty Cross-over Valve** — Perform Hydraulic Cross-over Check  
- **Internal Door Fault** — Mechanical inspection required
