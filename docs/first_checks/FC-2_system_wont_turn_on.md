# First Check — SYSTEM WON’T TURN ON  
*(Hydraulic Pump Won’t Run)*

**Source:** troubleshooting_guide.pdf  
**Document:** McDav-FFTTG  
**Revision:** 03  
**Pages:** 14  

---

## Symptom
- System will not turn on  
- Hydraulic pump does not run  

---

## Preconditions / Notes
- Control Box ON/OFF switch set to **ON**  
- All milspec plugs correctly connected  

---

## Decision Steps

### Step 1 — Display Check
**Question:**  
Are any LEDs illuminated on the Isolair Display Box?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 2  
- **NO →** Proceed to Step 3  

---

### Step 2 — Jettison Switch Check
**Action:**  
Toggle the Jettison Switch several times and ensure it is in the normal operating position (**UP**).

**Question:**  
Does the system now turn on?

- **YES →**
  - Jettison Switch was OFF or intermittently failing  
  - Replace Cyclic Controller  

- **NO →** Proceed to Step 4  

---

### Step 3 — Circuit Breaker Check
**Question:**  
Have any circuit breakers on the Isolair Display Box tripped?

- **YES →**
  - Reset circuit breakers and re-test system  
  - If system still does not turn on, proceed to Step 5  

- **NO →** Proceed to Step 7  

---

### Step 4 — Cyclic Controller Substitution
**Action:**  
Change the Cyclic Controller.

**Question:**  
Does the system now turn on?

- **YES →**
  - Faulty Cyclic Controller  
  - Tag faulty unit for workshop repair  
  - Identify failed internal switch during bench repair  

- **NO →** Proceed to Step 6  

---

### Step 5 — Aircraft Power Check
**Question:**  
Are you operating on ground power with engines not running?

- **YES →** Proceed to Step 7  

- **NO →**
  - Check all relevant aircraft circuit breakers  
  - Begin detailed troubleshooting at **Circuit Breaker Checks** (through checks)

---

### Step 6 — Pressure Switch Check
**Action:**  
Change the Pressure Switch.

**Question:**  
Does the system now turn on?

- **YES →**
  - Faulty Pressure Switch identified  
  - Replace Pressure Switch  

- **NO →**
  - Wiring or relay fault likely  
  - Begin detailed troubleshooting at **Tank Power Supply Check**

---

### Step 7 — Bus Power Check
**Action:**  
Ensure the **NON-ESSENTIAL BUS** is set to **MANUAL**.

> **NOTE**  
> On Bell 214 aircraft, MANUAL must be selected when operating from ground power.

**Question:**  
Does the system now turn on?

- **YES →**
  - Incorrect bus selection identified  

- **NO →**
  - Check all relevant aircraft circuit breakers  
  - Begin detailed troubleshooting at **Circuit Breaker Checks**

---

## Outcomes & Diagnoses
- **Faulty Circuit Breakers** — Perform Circuit Breaker Checks  
- **Faulty Cyclic Controller** — Replace Cyclic Controller  
- **Faulty Pressure Switch** — Perform Tank Power Supply Check  
- **Faulty Hydraulic Pump Power Relay** — Perform Hydraulic Pump Power Relay Check  
- **Wiring fault** — Perform Wiring Continuity Checks
